Tuesday, February 17, 2026 • 12:00–12:50 pm Eastern Time
Heather Bolles (Iowa State University): Integrating AI Tools in Calculus: Student Exploration, Evaluation, and Curriculum Insights
Abstract: This presentation describes a project integrating AI tools (LLMs and CASs) into calculus and differential equations through materials and assessments that prompt students to solve problems, engineer prompts, interpret results, and evaluate AI outputs. It reports student outcomes, survey feedback from instructors and students, and recommendations for curriculum improvement.
Tuesday, March 3, 2026 • 12:00–12:50 pm Eastern Time
Widodo Samyono (Jarvis Christian University): Enhancing Proof-Based Learning in Real Analysis through AI-Driven Inquiry-Based Learning
Abstract: This talk explores the integration of Generative AI in Inquiry-Based Learning (IBL) to teach proof-based Real Analysis. By leveraging AI tools, students engage deeply with complex mathematical concepts, fostering critical thinking and problem-solving skills. Our approach utilizes a lecture note on Introduction to Real Analysis from the IBL Journal, aligning teaching strategies with student learning outcomes. We will discuss the benefits and challenges of this innovative method, highlighting its potential to personalize learning experiences and improve student outcomes.
